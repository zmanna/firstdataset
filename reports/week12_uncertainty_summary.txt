Plain-language interpretation:
  - Uncertainty helps most when wrong predictions are noticeably less confident than correct ones. In this run, the strongest separation was for full_enhanced.
  - The model is easiest to trust when both calibration and selective prediction improve together. Here that points most strongly to top_ranked for calibration and top_ranked when keeping only the most confident predictions.
  - Reduced hybrid versus full enhanced: reduced_hybrid accuracy=0.8607, brier=0.1048; full_enhanced accuracy=0.8711, brier=0.0950.
  - Cross-environment failure should ideally come with higher uncertainty. Where incorrect cross-environment predictions still have high confidence, that is a sign of overconfidence under distribution shift. The clearest case here was proxy_only.
